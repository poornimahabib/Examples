ABOUT/

Sexual Harassment has been outlawed by the Govt. of India in 2013, through SEXUAL HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT WORKPLACE, (PREVENTION, PROHIBITION AND REDRESSAL) ACT, 2013. The Act states that Sexual Harassment results in violation of the fundamental rights of a woman to equality under Article 14 and 15 of the Constitution of India and her right to life and live with dignity under article 21 of the constitution. Any organization with ten or more employees need to set up an Internal Committee (IC). It is mandatory that there are awareness sessions for men and women, IC is skilled and trained and then the reporting in the annual report as well as to the District officer is timely. Many organizations use the checklist approach which is not aimed at really bringing the desired change. This is because most often organizations don’t know how to start and to work on the Act, and many a times, it is not an organizational priority.
